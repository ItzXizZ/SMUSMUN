General Assembly (GA)

Topic: Illicit Firearm Trafficking

Illicit weapon trafficking fuels violence, destabilizes nations, and enables crime and terrorism. This transnational issue, exacerbated by weak borders and corruption, requires global cooperation to address. Regions like Libya, Sudan, Syria, Yemen, Mexico, and Colombia are key hubs, with major arms producers like the U.S., Russia, and China fueling illegal markets.

The UN and DISEC lead efforts with initiatives like the Arms Trade Treaty, but enforcement gaps remain. Tackling this crisis demands international cooperation and pragmatic solutions for global security.

Crisis Committee

Topic: Russian Revolution

The Russian Revolution of 1917 shattered the Romanov dynasty and plunged Russia into political and social turmoil. As the Provisional Government struggles to maintain order, Bolsheviks, Mensheviks, monarchists, and other factions compete for control of the nation's future.

In this committee, delegates will navigate a rapidly changing landscape, debating issues like governance, war, and economic collapse. Will you lead Russia toward socialism, restore the monarchy, or forge a new path? With revolution at its peak, every decision will shape the fate of an empire.

Regional Council A

Topic: South China Sea

The South China Sea dispute has become a major issue for the U.S., with the Senate working to counter Chinese aggression while avoiding war or economic instability. Most senators reject China's Nine-Dash Line and support regional allies, but they differ on how to respond.

U.S. military alliances with ASEAN nations are also crucial as China expands its military power. As China's influence grows, the Senate must balance economic ties with protecting democratic allies, making its decisions critical to regional stability and global security.

NATO

Topic: Hybrid Threats

Strengthening resilience to hybrid threats is crucial to safeguarding the security of member states. As adversaries employ tactics across air, land, water, cyber, and space domains, NATO must evolve to effectively counter these multi-dimensional risks. While progress has been made, the growing sophistication of hybrid threats requires continued innovation and adaptation within NATO's strategic framework.

NATO's success in countering hybrid threats will depend on unified action among members, ensuring readiness and resilience in the face of an increasingly complex security landscape.

House of Commons (HOC)

Topic: Canada's Defense Spending

Canada's defense and military spending is essential for national security and fulfilling international obligations. With rising global tensions, there is a need to balance defense funding with other priorities like healthcare and social programs.

However, Canada's defense budget remains lower than many allies, raising questions about its military readiness. The House of Commons must consider increasing defense investment to ensure Canada can effectively address emerging threats and maintain a strong role on the global stage.
